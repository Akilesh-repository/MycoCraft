Category 01

General Cultivation & Kit Basics

What is inside an All-In-One Mushroom Grow Bag?
Our All-In-One bags feature perfectly layered components: clean, sterilized organic rye berries at the base for initial colonization, and our field-capacity CVG (Coco Coir, Vermiculite, Gypsum) substrate mix on top. This clean architecture isolates the grains until the mycelium structure stabilizes.
How long can I store an un-inoculated grow kit safely?
Thanks to our rigorous high-pressure laboratory sterilization, un-inoculated grow bags maintain structural purity for up to 3 to 4 months. For ideal durability, warehouse them inside a cold, dark, and dry climate enclosure away from volatile ambient exposures.
What temperature window should I maintain during colonization?
Specialty and gourmet mushroom strains expand aggressively when maintained inside a dependable heat corridor of 72°F to 78°F (22°C to 26°C). Avoid driving temperatures beyond 82°F, as elevated heat corridors increase contamination vulnerability and stall active colonization lines.
Do I absolutely require an external fruiting chamber or tent?
No, an external tent setup is not mandatory. Our specialized bags are engineered as standalone micro-climates. You can trigger fruit development natively inside the bag structure via simple cutting and manual misting, though deploying monotubs or climate enclosures can boost flush volume.
How many flushes can I secure from a single grow kit block?
A clean, healthy block routinely supports 2 to 3 complete cropping flushes before depleting internal resources. The first flush delivers the highest density, after which you must cleanly rehydrate the blocks via dunking procedures to prepare the mycelium for subsequent flushes.
Category 02

Advanced Lab Work & Sterile Procedures

What is the shelf life of isolated liquid culture syringes?
When carefully refrigerated between 35°F and 40°F (2°C to 4°C), our live isolated liquid cultures preserve absolute structural integrity for 6 to 9 months. Ensure the storage chamber never dips below freezing, as solid ice crystal formulation fractures live cell membranes.
What are the exact sterilization parameters for your substrates?
All organic grain spawns and bulk substrate formulations process through commercial-grade autoclaves locked down at 15 PSI. This pressure environment maintains a core sterilization temperature of 250°F (121°C) for a strict minimum cycle duration of 150 minutes.
What micron rating do your bag filter patches feature?
Our premium high-durability bags utilize integrated 0.2-micron gas-exchange filter patches. This fine-weave scale completely prevents wild airborne mold spores, competing bacteria strands, and fine dust particulates from accessing clean substrate environments.
How should I cleanly clone a wild mushroom tissue to an agar plate?
Always operate inside a calibrated Class 100 laminar flow hood or a sanitized still-air box setup. Flame-sterilize your laboratory scalpel till red hot, slice open a clean section of the fresh mushroom cap or stem, extract an inner tissue sample, and carefully seat it onto the agar.
What nutritional media recipe is used in pre-poured plates?
Our pre-poured laboratory petri dishes utilize premium standard Malt Extract Agar (MEA) compound, carefully enriched with highly refined nutritional organic yeast powder. This blend supplies vital base sugars and minerals that trigger high-speed rhizomorphic mycelium branching.
